About

The reasoning behind the work.

Most graph mining research trades exactness for speed. I work on closing that gap — finding the real answer, not an approximation, at the scale that modern hardware makes possible.

Right now that means GPU-accelerated algorithms for dense subgraph discovery: densest subgraph, quasi-cliques, k-plex enumeration. These are problems where the combinatorial structure of the pruning strategy and the physical layout of data in GPU memory both determine whether you finish in seconds or never.

Before research became my main focus, I spent two years in cloud engineering at Deloitte USI — building production integrations and data pipelines for enterprise clients. That work taught me to care about correctness under real-world constraints, not just clean theoretical settings. I still carry that bias.
