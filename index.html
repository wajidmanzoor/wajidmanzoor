About

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Data Science at Rowan University, advised by Dr. Guimu Guo. My research focuses on GPU-accelerated exact algorithms for dense subgraph problems — densest subgraph discovery, maximal quasi-clique mining, and k-plex enumeration.

What draws me to these problems is that correctness matters: most approaches trade accuracy for speed, and I'm interested in closing that gap using GPU parallelism rather than approximation.

Before starting my Ph.D., I worked as a Business Analyst in cloud engineering at Deloitte USI, building data integrations and pipelines for enterprise clients including 10x Genomics and Gilead Sciences. That background shapes how I think about systems: algorithms should work on real data, not just clean benchmarks.

Research

01

Exact GPU Graph Mining

Algorithms that maintain exactness while fully exploiting GPU thread hierarchies and warp-level parallelism. The goal is the real answer, on hardware fast enough for it to matter.

02

Dense Structure Discovery

Densest subgraphs, maximal quasi-cliques, and k-plex enumeration — clique relaxation problems where combinatorial pruning and GPU memory coalescing are equally load-bearing.

03

Algorithms Meets Hardware

A foundation in optimization, machine learning, and scientific computing — the bridge between theoretical guarantees and systems that actually run at scale.
